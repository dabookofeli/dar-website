Neutral infrastructure primitive

Verifiable receipts for digital actions.

Digital Action Receipts (DARs) are issued at the moment an action occurs and preserved as append-only, tamper-evident records. They provide a durable reference to what happened—before disputes, audits, incident reviews, or narrative reconstruction.

DAR = a verifiable, append-only record of a digital action, issued at execution time. It preserves: actor · action · object · timestamp · reference · policy/context (optional) · integrity proof

Orientation

What DAR is

A small, durable artifact that proves an action occurred. DAR records facts, not interpretations.

append-only tamper-evident verifiable

What DAR is not

Not monitoring. Not dashboards. Not policy enforcement. Not a compliance product. It preserves evidence.

no alerts no scoring no interpretation

Why it exists

When systems are questioned later, mutable logs and narratives fail. DAR provides a stable reference that survives remediation and hindsight.

disputes audits incidents

How DAR fits into existing systems

DAR is designed to be added with minimal coupling: issue a receipt where an action is committed; store it append-only; verify it later by reference. It complements logs and observability by preserving an immutable anchor for the most consequential actions.

  • Write-heavy, read-light: optimized for issuance at scale and verification on demand.
  • No runtime ML inference: receipts are evidence artifacts, not predictions.
  • Neutral boundary: provides proof of action without becoming the arbiter of disputes.
