Customer-owned proof of digital actions

Proof that an action occurred — independent, verifiable, and durable.

Digital Action Receipts (DAR) are cryptographically verifiable, append-only evidence that an action occurred at execution time, preserved in customer-controlled immutable storage.

Request Integration Access Read the Definition

DAR is not monitoring, logging, or observability. It’s evidence designed for audit, dispute, and continuity.

What DAR provides

Receipts bind key facts at action time so you can prove what happened later.

Evidence primitive
Signed receipt at the action boundary (commit time)
Customer custody
Stored in customer-controlled immutable storage
Independent verification
Verify without trusting the application that produced the action

How it works (conceptual)

Integrators issue a receipt after a meaningful action completes, then persist it immutably. Implementation details (schemas, SDKs, endpoints) are provided in gated documentation after access approval.

1
Action completes
A system, user, or agent performs a meaningful operation.
2
Receipt is issued
A receipt is generated and cryptographically signed at execution time.
3
Receipt is archived
Your system persists the receipt to customer-controlled immutable storage.
4
Receipt is verified
Later, the receipt can be verified independently for audit or dispute.
Storage responsibility: DAR returns a signed receipt. Your system archives it. DAR does not write into customer infrastructure by default.

What DAR is for

DAR is designed for systems where trust, continuity, and accountability matter — especially when actions are executed by software, agents, or distributed teams.

Audit continuity

Provide durable evidence of what occurred, even when systems change or logs are incomplete.

Dispute evidence

Resolve “what happened?” by referencing independently verifiable receipts.

Agent actions

Bind AI/agent tool calls and outcomes to proof at execution time without relying on narrative logs.
