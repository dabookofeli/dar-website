Neutral infrastructure primitive

The receipt is the authority for digital actions.

Digital Action Receipts (DARs) are issued at action time and preserved as append-only, tamper-evident evidence in customer-controlled storage. When systems are questioned later, logs and narratives drift. Receipts establish what happened.

DAR, in one sentence

A DAR is a small, durable artifact that proves a specific action occurred — by a specific actor — against a specific object — at a specific time — under a specific policy/config context.

DAR = verifiable, append-only evidence of a digital action, issued at execution time. Preserves: actor · action · object · timestamp · stable reference · policy/context (optional) · integrity proof
append-only tamper-evident independently verifiable customer custody

Dead-simple integration (concept)

Issue a receipt at the action boundary and attach a stable reference. Store it append-only. Verify later.

await issueReceipt({ actor: "system|user|agent", action: "approved", object: "loan_application", ref: "internal-id-123", policy_version_ref: "policy-2026-01", model_version_hash: "sha256:…", config_ref: "cfg-9a2f…" })

Write-heavy, read-light. Optimized for issuance at scale and verification on demand.

Why this philosophy matters

In disputes, audits, and investigations, the system is not the witness. The receipt is.
DAR does not replace systems of record. DAR replaces reconstruction. It preserves facts at the moment of action — without monitoring, interpreting, or enforcing behavior.

Universal Evidence Layer

DAR sits beneath many systems as a shared evidence fabric — quietly recording what happened, when it happened, and under what context — without controlling decisions.

View diagram
Customer-controlled storage. Receipts store references (policy versions, model hashes, config refs) — not raw data.

How DAR fits into existing systems

  • Receipts are issued at action time, not reconstructed later.
  • Receipts are stored in customer-owned infrastructure (immutable / append-only).
  • Unlike logs, receipts are independently verifiable and portable.
  • No surveillance. No monitoring. No decision logic. No enforcement.
