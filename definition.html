Foundational definition

What is a Digital Action Receipt?

Digital Action Receipts (DARs) are neutral, verifiable records of digital actions, issued at the moment an action occurs and preserved as append-only, tamper-evident evidence.

A Digital Action Receipt is a cryptographically verifiable record issued at execution time that attests a specific actor performed a specific digital action against a specific object, at a specific moment, under an identifiable context.

Why Digital Action Receipts exist

Most systems rely on mutable logs, dashboards, or reconstructed narratives to explain what happened after an incident, dispute, or audit begins. These mechanisms are inherently fragile: they can be altered, re-exported, or reinterpreted under pressure.

DAR exists to preserve a **durable factual anchor** — what actually happened at the moment of action — before explanations, remediation, or hindsight enter the picture.

Core properties

Append-only

Once issued, a receipt cannot be modified or erased. Corrections and remediation produce new receipts rather than altering history.

Tamper-evident

Any attempt to alter a receipt or its sequence is detectable through cryptographic verification.

Execution-time issuance

Receipts are created at the moment the action is executed — not during later analysis or reporting.

Neutral by design

DAR records facts, not intent, correctness, risk scoring, or interpretation.

What a receipt contains

A DAR captures the minimum information necessary to attest that an action occurred, while remaining portable across systems and domains.

{ "actor": "system | user | agent", "action": "approved | denied | executed | invoked", "object": "resource | transaction | policy | tool", "timestamp": "ISO-8601", "reference": "stable-internal-id", "context": "policy/version/environment (optional)", "integrity": "cryptographic proof" }

What DAR is not

Not monitoring or surveillance

DAR does not observe behavior continuously or infer intent.

Not a decision engine

DAR does not approve, deny, score, or enforce policy.

Not a dashboard or log replacement

DAR complements existing systems by preserving immutable facts.

Not an arbiter of disputes

DAR provides evidence; interpretation is left to auditors, regulators, courts, or customers.

Where DAR applies

Digital Action Receipts are not limited to a single industry or workflow. Their value emerges across multiple Action Domains, wherever digital actions must become durable facts.

Authoritative scope

DAR is intentionally narrow. Its purpose is to preserve historical truth at the boundary of action — not to control systems, assign blame, or replace governance.

This restraint is what allows DAR to function as neutral infrastructure rather than an opinionated product.
